Ehealth

Ehealth app provides easy mobile access to citizens‚Äô healthcare information and other health service. It also helps in creating comfortable and stable health care support.

Introduction

In this project, I redesigned a healthcare mobile application which should have served a lot of people in my hometown but actually failed without updates. My emphasis in this project was on improving the interface and making it more mobile friendly with easy-understanding task flows.

Type

Mobile APP UX Redesign (self-project)

Timeline

Sep - Dec 2017 (Refined in 2018)

My Role

User research & study, information architecture, personas, storymapping, prototyping, wireframing, quick user testing

Context & Problem

Ehealth app is the mobile derivative of an online web-based health management tool, and the company released the app about 6 years ago. However, it fails in helping users efficiently.

Thinking of you suppose that it is a mini-Baymax, but in fact, not. Complex information, unfriendly interactive ways and lacking of emotional visual design all persuade potential users from making it into a powerful healthcare assistant.

Redesign Goal

Key insights from my small-scale user research asked for 3 main improvements in new design where I started my redesign process.

‚Äúclear info presenting‚Äù

Related information should be organized for users to find things easily. Features which are not priority in mobile ones would be removed.

‚Äúeasy to understand‚Äù

Confusing or complex expressions should be avoided. Instructions should be provided for users with minimum user action steps.

‚Äúmobile friendly‚Äù

Instead of ‚Äúcopying+pasting‚Äù content and layouts almost the same from web side, a more mobile friendly version should be used in layouts, and in visual as well.

Information Architecture

In redesigning a new information architecture for the EHealth app, I tried to grouping related modules together, resetting hierarchy, and simplifying unnecessary information presenting.

What's New Here?

new interface style

Recent important events and reminders are now accessible in dashboard. From here, users could access to all other features or categories.

clear form

1 column makes things easier and clear for users compared to previous 2 columns forms. Hints included as text placeholder to provide immediate instructions for users.

reminders

Hints and call-to-action rules are now inside even visual elements. Different button styles demonstrate priority in calling users' attention.

wording

Users will see more friendly texts being used in this app to make it more like talking to someone instead of "follow the rule".

organized information

Card-style make it easier to identify items and provide a better space experience.

visual experience

Users can now check more lively information from the app, like they can now check real images of health service providers, and colors like green representing life is largely used apart from theme blue color.

Visual Design

Color & Typeface

In choosing color palettes, I selected blue as theme color with certain saturation which provided safety and softness for users. In my design, I included a feature that the system colors might be adjusted based on users‚Äô health information. For instance, for users who have hemophilia, red color series may not be favored by them and therefore, the color of promotion or alert styles they receive may be less red and more orange.

Icon Design

For icon design, I kept on the idea that creating a safe and warm interfaces for users. Therefore I avoided sharp corner and end point, with simple icons as well to decrease unnecessary information delivered to users compared to health info.

Final Interface Outputs

In final interface outputs, I chose those with higher priority and provided a high-fidelity mockups.

Research

App Critique

To get the idea of what the most up-to-date version of app looks like, I made an app critique first. And here shows some of the outcomes.

Comparative + User Study

To understand how other products sharing similar or partial features design a good user flow and figure out more potential improving space, I conducted a comparative analysis including 8 healthcare products. Based on my analysis, I came up with several key insights for products that are user-friendly which are similar to results of user interviews.

Together with the comparative study, to have redesign base on solid foundation from very beginning, I got interviews with people who had experience with it before to better understand users needs. And here came the key insights and my redesign goal.

Design Exploration

After determine overall app structure, I started to look into detailed process for each feature to prepare for design process. Story mapping allows me to go through potential user process as listing out each step and possible actions as many as possible.

Design synthesis & denfense allowed me to have more intuitive comparison when deciding better way for a user task.

Target Users

Busy young adults

Young adults users busy at work who usually consider location and time as the most important factors.

Mid-age residents

Users have lived in the city for a while and usually have their preferences about where to go for healthcare. more free time slots.

Children & Teenagers

Users have healthcare plans but need to receive care service with the approval and accompany of their parents.

Sketchings

During this iteration phase, more details were found like one of my tester mentioned that many people around her thought that a link among family members would be helpful, like parents could get access to children's health data.

Wireframes

In wireframing phase, I tried to come up with main features that would be frequently used on mobile side without going deeply into details. Several rounds of layout iterations were conducted to find better way for users to interact with.

Moodboard

In the original version, more than 10 colors shared similar area proportion appeared in a single interface, which made it looks like a water color set.In my exploration, I tried to focus on creating clean and safe context, while keeping the overall feeling a vivid style.

Reflection

As the project is based on limited research which might lead to bias in user study result (due to the project assignment's nature of going me through the whole process of interaction design), it is important to work with more researchers and the healthcare teams at the very beginning, if there is any future chances. Besides, in some interface design, the color chosen should be more careful as some types of patients do not favor the red-series of colors. Therefore, a more customized and flexible version should be also considerred in the future.
