Groove

Do you feel bored or want to make some social interactions when waiting for bus? Groove provides you a great opportunity to "create" your own music piece and collaborate with others.

Introduction

Groove is an interactive music composing bus stop, supported by advanced projecting and motion sensing technology. The goal of this design is to provide a joyful waiting experience and exiciting social chance through people adding sound pieces to background music and share happiness together. My contribution included mainly on user research and interface design.

Type

Interactive Installation Design

Timeline

OVERVIEW

Context

Bus as a public transport means plays a vital role in day to day activities. For instance, the bus is an important means of transport that assist the socially disadvantaged, alleviating traffic congestion and plays a crucial role in conserving the natural resources according to Graham Currie and Janet Stanley. 2008.

For most students, bus is seen as the most economic and convenient way of commuting as they spent certain amount of time on it everyday. However, negative waiting experience caused by long waiting time and impaired service provision keeps discouraging them.

A bus stop related products analysis was conducted to explore what other experiments have been carried out aimed at ensuring that the bus waiting experience becomes interesting. It was found that all the design aimed at supporting multi-personal participation and encourages interpersonal communication

User Research

To get an idea of what our target audience, university students, thought about waiting experience, we conducted field observation, online and onsite surveys, and user interviews.

We got a hours-long field observation record, 7 interview results and hundreds of survey responses. From these, it was revealed that negative feelings like "get bored", "become impatient" and "get uncomfortable" were what passengers had to bear with during waiting time in bus station.

4 key insights were summarized from these user research.

Long Waiting Time

Majority of survey participants felt that the waiting time was too long, which resulted in boredom and loneliness.

Little Interaction

People reluctant to start a conversation with strangers mainly due to introvert personality and interacting with strangers unpredictably.

Need Timely Bus Info

60% of survey participants considered getting reliable bus information was critical to determine a good bus waiting experience.

More than Bus

Apart from bus information, news, games or information aobut local cultures were favored as well in bus stops.

Design Goals

We set our overarching design goal as providing a joyful waiting experience in bus station and decomposed it into 3 sub-objectives.

Brainstorming

Time for brainstorming! During the 1st round, we tried to be as creative as possible and thought of nearly 20 ideas. Inspired by analysis from related products on market, we thought of combining more interesting interactions.

Based on a set of criteria including consideration about accessibility, safety, etc, we refined and combined advantages from our ideations, getting rid of complex story-based, highly immersive and physical intensive ones. Then the second round of brainstorming was conducted and resulted in a final idea of creating collaborating music composing interaction device inside the bus stop. In this regards, we made paper and low fidelity digital prototypes and tested them inside school public building.

It finally came out our design solution, Groove, a collaborative music compositing facility inside bus station as our solution.
