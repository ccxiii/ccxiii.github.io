Overview

Alcohol addiction is a widespread issue in current society. In the US, there are about 15.1 million people who suffer from alcohol disorder. There is also a statistic data of 6 deaths per day due to alcohol poisoning. It is also a hard-to-break habit due to various reasons as it helps make someone more outgoing, helping stressful people relax, assisting in better social networking, etc. Irrespective of its benefits many people try to control their consumption of alcohol based on health consideration but it is super hard to get rid of overconsumption successfully.​​​​​​​​

​​​​​​Thus we designed LIME, a smart glass to help drinkers control their drinking and therefore increase their health awareness. It also helps build a safe environment for the users and the people around them and makes people adopt healthier habits and lead productive lives.

User Research

Research Questions

To understand the drinking behavior of young people from research, we firstly set up several research questions which guided our later study process.

"how?"

What are the drinking patterns of the young and if they control their drinking habits

"what?"

In what conditions and environments do people prefer drinking?

"why?"

Why do people drink and not drink alcohol? What do they think about it?​​​

Research Study

Based on our questions, we conducted a series of formative studies in 3 weeks: online surveys, diary studies (conducted by my teammates), and user interviews, with the last two based on online survey analysis.

From online survey, which included 192 responses, we got 3 basic insights.

Socialize: the key motivation

Most of the people go to a bar with friends and drink so that they can be socially accepted in the group.

Over-consumption is hard to control

83.33% have been victims of over-consumption of alcohol and from that 45% are trying hard to control their the alcohol intake.

Health concern leads to alcohol control

most of the people try to avoid or control alcohol due to issues related to personal health like losing weight, healthier life style, etc.​​

Based on the survey results, conducted by my teammates, in the diary studies, we provided 2 participants with sets of dairy kit including recording cards, hint cards (with text reminders trying to remind participants of other important things instead of alcohol) and a dozen of alcohols. The goal of this study was to keep track of what types of hints or reminders would be useful in helping drinkers and if the hints are useful.

card for dairy study, which our testers needed to check 1 new card every time when they wanted to drink

Together with user interviews after the studies, we summarized our final user research insights in 4 main aspects.

Key Insights

People are trying to control alcohol consumption as they are becoming more health-conscious, but there are not efficient technology now to stop drinking while people drinks.

Over-consumption is common among drinkers which will result in safety issues. The lacking of self-consious awareness and no way to provide accurate information like blood alcohol content data during the process, also exagerate the situation.

If there is a way to help drinkers, it may affect restaurants revene, or decrease bars' income. Inaccurate alcohol detection or frequent reminders may be problematic or annoying.

A stopper for drinkers will help them control addiction, make people more productive, promote healthy lives, as well as creating a safe environment for the community.

Prototyping

To think in a more logicla and broader scope, we applied "Speed Dating Matrix" method to analyze the potential situations first before we came up with even more ideas generated from the 3 selected categories.

Refining our ideas into more available ones in 2nd round of brainstorming, to get a deeper understanding about how user thought about our ideas and make further refining, we recruited participants for 7 user enactments with varying drinking frequencies. For every protoype, we created a scenario like general scene in bar around campus, to help user feel in right the situation and respond as they would in real cases.

User Enactments

During our testing process, we made records and refinements which finally becoming the analysis of enactment process

With modifications during the user enactment process, we finally got our 4 key testing insights.

Visual language is more efficient in calling one’s attention. Information presented in the message should be clear and easily-understood for ordinary people.

In our enactments, users preferred scenarios which reminded them about their current situations and making recommendations, rather than performing actions for users automatically.

Users want to make their own decisions, with information provided in proper way as references.

Motion effect attached to device like mug holded by users could be helpful in assisting increasing users’ awareness of reminders especially in the environment when it is noisy and the light condition is unpredictable.​​

Winner from Enactments

We narrowed down based on these results, using a matrix which rated our improvised concepts based on effective, enjoyable, acceptable, trust-worthy, and plausible. With which, we picked out beneficial features from several ideas and refined them into our final design, Lime.

Final System Proposal

Our final design, named as "Lime", consists of a glass which uses persuasion techniques to reduce alcohol consumption of users in the context of a bar.

Our system concept involves the glass with sensors that are used in a bar setting. The interaction between these elements summarized in the diagram below.

The three main features of our product are based on these two motivations to stop drinking. Our product has a screen which alerts users about the number of drinks the user has consumed and the effects it has on his body. We have used haptic feedback for the user to alert him through vibration every time his blood alcohol level changes.

The second feature detects the alcohol level and displays the computed blood alcohol level and its effect on the screen to make the user more aware of the effects the alcohol is having on them. From our research, it was found that self-awareness is one of the major factors for the users to stop drinking alcohol which we have tried to address through the above 2 features.

The other feature of our product is the water compartment in the glass. When the user is drinking too fast, the screen will prompt the user to drink water and be hydrated which keeps the user in better health even if he drinks water when he is drinking alcohol. This will also get him full discouraging more alcohol consumption.

To make the interactive prototype, my 2 teammates Kesava and Rugved used ubicomp electronic set in creating the features in wood craft models, with a conceptual glass model made by 3D printing.

Reflection

Overall, our demo provided audiences a basic understanding about how this product worked and the benefits it brought to people's real life. More issues should be considered in the future, especially how to make the alcohol detection correctly without the influence of alcohol inside the glass (we had the detector in outside of the glass but there could still be errors in detecting).
